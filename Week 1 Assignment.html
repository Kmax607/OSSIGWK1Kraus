Chapter 1, Extend Your Knowledge

Guideline 1.1 - Text Alternatives: This guideline ensures that all non-text content has a text alternative that serves the equivalent purpose. This is crucial for users who rely on screen readers or other assistive technologies to understand the content of images, videos, and other multimedia elements.

Guideline 1.2 - Time-based Media: This guideline focuses on providing alternatives for time-based media, such as audio and video. This includes captions for videos, text descriptions for audio content, and transcripts for multimedia elements, making the content accessible to people with hearing or visual impairments.

Guideline 1.3 - Adaptable: Content must be created in a way that it can be presented in different ways without losing information or structure. This means using proper HTML markup to ensure that content can be adapted to different devices and assistive technologies.

Guideline 1.4 - Distinguishable: This guideline emphasizes making it easier for users to see and hear content, including separating foreground from background. It includes recommendations for using sufficient contrast, not relying solely on color to convey information, and ensuring that audio content is clear and distinguishable.
